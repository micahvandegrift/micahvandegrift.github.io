Biographical sketch:

Micah Vandegrift is a strategic leader and innovator dedicated to building and guiding high-performing teams that transform complex strategy into actionable solutions across the science, technology, and research sectors. As a Client Configuration and Support Lead at Digital Science, he applies his expertise in user-centered design and digital infrastructure to drive business efficiency. He has initiated AI integrations that improved team efficiency and AI utility by 75% and streamlined client onboarding processes for 35% faster completion.

Micah’s unique career is rooted in maximizing the accessibility and public impact of knowledge. He previously served as a Senior Strategist and Team Lead for the NIH’s All of Us Research Program, where he directed a multidisciplinary strategy team to pinpoint user pain points, resulting in a 45% reduction in onboarding time. Prior to this, he was NC State University’s Open Knowledge Librarian and the Lead Principal Investigator for a $414,000 grant from the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation, pioneering a tech accelerator that launched 50% of incubated projects. He also served as a Fulbright Scholar in Europe, studying open science policy and infrastructure.

Earlier in his career, Micah founded and directed the Office of Digital Research and Scholarship at Florida State University (FSU), growing and leading a team of seven new employees and establishing an innovative service portfolio.

Micah holds a Master of Library and Information Studies (M.L.I.S) and an M.A. in American and Florida Studies, both from FSU. Based in Raleigh, North Carolina, he enjoys soccer, camping, and music, and has proudly competed in the World Beard and Mustache Championships.
